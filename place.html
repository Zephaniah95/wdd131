History

Nigeria's history dates back to the Nok civilization, which thrived from 1500 BC. The country was a major hub for trade and powerful kingdoms like the Oyo Empire and Benin Kingdom. British colonial rule began in the 19th century, and the Northern and Southern Protectorates were amalgamated in 1914. Nigeria gained independence on October 1, 1960.

Culture

Nigeria is a diverse country with over 250 ethnic groups and more than 500 languages. The three largest ethnic groups are the Hausa-Fulani, Igbo, and Yoruba. These groups have distinct traditions, food, and clothing styles. Nigeria is also a global cultural powerhouse, home to the world's second-largest film industry, Nollywood. Jollof rice is a popular national dish.
